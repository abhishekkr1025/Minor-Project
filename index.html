Speed Control of DC Motor

Sometimes, we want to control the speed of a DC motor on demand. This intentional change of drive speed is known as speed control of a DC motor.

Speed control of a DC motor is either done manually by the operator or by means of an automatic control device. This is different to speed regulation – where the speed is trying to be maintained (or ‘regulated’) against the natural change in speed due to a change in the load on the shaft

The three methods of speed control are

1.Armature Voltage Control Method

2.Armature resistance Control Method

3.Flux Control Method

TRIAC

TRIAC is an acronym that stands for “Triode for Alternating Current”. Triode means a three-terminal device while AC means that it is used for switching Alternating current. It is a three-terminal bi-directional switch that conducts in both directions. It is made from the combination of two SCRs in anti-parallel with their gates joined together.
